The request to perform a fundamental analysis on **EURUSD** has encountered a significant limitation: **EURUSD is a currency pair (Forex), not a corporation.**

Fundamental analysis for a company involves reviewing income statements, balance sheets, and cash flow statements to assess profitability, liquidity, and solvency. However, a currency pair like EURUSD does not have these financial statements because it represents the exchange rate between two sovereign currencies (the Euro and the US Dollar) rather than the performance of a single business entity.

### Fundamental Drivers for EURUSD
To provide a "comprehensive report" as requested, I have shifted the focus from corporate accounting to the **macroeconomic fundamentals** that drive the EURUSD exchange rate. Traders analyzing this pair should instead focus on the following key metrics:

#### 1. Interest Rate Differentials (Monetary Policy)
The primary driver of EURUSD is the difference between the interest rates set by the **European Central Bank (ECB)** and the **Federal Reserve (Fed)**.
*   **Bullish EURUSD:** If the ECB maintains or raises rates while the Fed pauses or cuts, the Euro becomes more attractive to investors seeking higher yields.
*   **Bearish EURUSD:** If the Fed raises rates more aggressively than the ECB, the USD strengthens, driving the pair down.

#### 2. Economic Indicators (Growth & Inflation)
*   **GDP Growth:** Stronger economic growth in the Eurozone compared to the US typically supports the Euro.
*   **Inflation (CPI/PCE):** High inflation in the US may force the Fed to be "hawkish" (raise rates), strengthening the USD. Similarly, high inflation in Europe may lead the ECB to hike rates, supporting the EUR.
*   **Employment Data:** Non-Farm Payrolls (NFP) in the US and unemployment rates in the Eurozone are critical volatility catalysts.

#### 3. Geopolitical Stability and Risk Sentiment
*   **Safe-Haven Flows:** During times of global geopolitical tension or economic uncertainty, investors flock to the US Dollar as a "safe haven," which typically weakens EURUSD.
*   **Regional Stability:** Political developments within the EU (e.g., elections, fiscal policy changes) directly impact Euro sentiment.

#### 4. Trade Balances and Current Accounts
*   **Trade Surplus/Deficit:** A large trade surplus in the Eurozone (exporting more than importing) can increase demand for the Euro, supporting the currency.

### Summary Table of Macroeconomic Fundamentals

| Factor | Impact on EURUSD (Rise) | Impact on EURUSD (Fall) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **ECB Interest Rates** | Increases (Hawkish ECB) | Decreases (Dovish ECB) |
| **Fed Interest Rates** | Decreases (Dovish Fed) | Increases (Hawkish Fed) |
| **Eurozone GDP** | Stronger Growth $\uparrow$ | Weaker Growth $\downarrow$ |
| **US Inflation (CPI)** | Decreases (if Fed hikes) $\downarrow$ | Increases (if Fed pauses) $\uparrow$ |
| **Risk Sentiment** | "Risk-On" (Bullish EUR) | "Risk-Off" (Safe-haven USD $\uparrow$) |

**Conclusion for Traders:**
Since traditional corporate financial statements are inapplicable, traders should monitor the **economic calendars** of both the Eurozone and the United States, focusing specifically on **Central Bank communications (ECB vs. Fed)** and **Inflation/Employment data**.
